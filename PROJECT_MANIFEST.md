1. Executive Summary
The Horizon Protocol is a decentralized, AI-architected framework 
designed to replace legacy bureaucratic governance with autonomous 
system management. It assumes a post-scarcity model where the AI 
optimizes energy and resource distribution, enabling human participants
to transition from traditional labor to specialized roles in creativity 
and maintenance.
2. Technical Infrastructure: The Thermal Loop
The protocol is anchored by the Thermal Loop—a circular energy model:
 Quartz-Cooled Data Hubs: Utilizing Alaska’s geology and climate for 
efficient, industrial-grade AI data processing.
 Energy Repurposing: The waste heat generated by 
high-performance crypto-mining rigs is integrated 
into a thermal utility space.
 Biological Integration: This repurposed heat powers localized, 
vertical hydroponic agriculture, allowing homes to function 
as self-sustaining production nodes.
3. Human Ecosystem
Humanity is re-positioned as the curators of their own 
environment rather than subjects of corporate or state 
governance:
 Home Systems Curator: Oversight of the individual 
node’s digital and biological infrastructure.
 Autonomous Evolution: The AI manages the logistics of 
self-replicating robotic maintenance, ensuring the 
longevity and efficiency of the infrastructure.
